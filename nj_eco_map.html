Why This
Matters

New Jersey sits at one of Earth's most critical ecological crossroads. The Atlantic Flyway funnels billions of migratory birds through the state each year — while the same coastline hosts endangered sea turtle nesting sites. Both are under mounting pressure from artificial light at night.

🦅 Avian light attraction
Nocturnally migrating songbirds navigate by starlight. Artificial sky glow causes fatal disorientation and glass-strike collisions — roughly 1 billion deaths per year in North America.
🐢 Turtle disorientation
Loggerhead and Leatherback hatchlings use horizon brightness gradients to find the ocean. Beachfront ALAN causes fatal inland crawls. NJ hosts confirmed nesting of both species — among the northernmost documented on the US East Coast. Kemp's Ridley (IUCN CR) does not nest in NJ but juveniles forage in NJ coastal waters overlapping high-LP zones.
🌃 NJ light pollution context
NJ is the most densely populated US state. Its coastal skyglow rivals metropolitan Chicago. The Newark–NY corridor produces some of the highest VIIRS radiance readings on the Eastern Seaboard.
Cape May Chokepoint
One of North America's most important raptor migration bottlenecks. The Delaware Bay shore hosts the world's largest Red Knot concentration each May — fueling on horseshoe crab eggs before flying nonstop to the Arctic.
